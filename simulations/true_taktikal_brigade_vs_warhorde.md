# True Taktikal Brigade vs War Horde

## Why This Exists

The earlier `Taktikal Brigade` recommendation blurred two different questions:

1. is the roster good?
2. is the `Taktikal Brigade` detachment actually the right home for that roster?

That was a fair callout.

A real `Taktikal Brigade` list should meaningfully exploit:

- `Lissen 'Ere`
- `Fight Proppa`
- `Taktikal Retreat`
- `On To Da Next`
- `Ded Sneaky`

If the list is mostly just `Ghaz`, `Nobz`, `Meganobz`, and straight-line pressure, then `War Horde` is usually the cleaner answer.

## Test Goal

This pass compares:

- a `true` `Taktikal Brigade` build
- a comparable `War Horde` pressure build

against the same Necron matchup family.

## What Each Detachment Gains

### Taktikal Brigade

- better utility from `Kommandos` and `Stormboyz`
- more reward for spreading threat pieces
- more value from fall back / move / reserve tricks
- better mission play on wide and action-heavy maps

### War Horde

- better universal melee output
- better payoff on `Nobz`, `Meganobz`, `Boyz`, and `Ghaz`
- less setup burden
- easier conversion from “made contact” to “dead Necron unit”

## What This Shows

Your instinct was right.

The earlier `Taktikal Brigade` result was flattering the roster concept more than the detachment fit.

Once the lists are built honestly:

- `Taktikal Brigade` is real
- `Taktikal Brigade` is not bad
- `Taktikal Brigade` is not the best answer overall into this Necron matchup

`War Horde` comes back out ahead.

## Where Taktikal Brigade Is Better

It does have real upsides.

It is better on:

- `Terraform`
- wide `Supply Drop` style missions
- games where repeated side pressure matters more than cracking the center

Why:

- `Kommandos` and `Stormboyz` can keep creating mission problems
- the Necron castle is not great at answering multiple lateral threats forever
- the detachment tricks matter more when the game is about movement and actions

## Where War Horde Is Better

It is better on:

- dense mid-board fight maps
- missions where you must actually remove durable defenders
- games where `The Silent King`, `Wraiths`, or `Triarch Stalkers` have to die on schedule

Why:

- `War Horde` gives broader value to the units that actually win those fights
- the list is less fiddly
- its threat conversion is more reliable

## Corrected Ranking

Against this Necron army, the corrected ranking is:

1. `War Horde`
2. `Taktikal Brigade`
3. `Green Tide`

If the opponent is the more extreme `triple 3x Lokhust` version, this same ordering still makes sense.

## Practical Conclusion

If you want the strongest all-round Ork answer:

- play `War Horde`

If you want a valid alternative that wins more through mission pressure and side-angle play:

- play `Taktikal Brigade`

But it should be a genuinely detachment-driven build, not just a War Horde list renamed.

## Bottom Line

`Taktikal Brigade` was worth testing, and your skepticism was justified.

For the actual matchup we’ve been studying, `War Horde` remains the better detachment overall.
