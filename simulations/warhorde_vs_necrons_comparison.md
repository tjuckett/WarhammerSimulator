## Target Necron List

This comparison is built to fight the Necron roster from `lists/necrons/Necrons  - Cursed Legion.json`, which includes:

- The Silent King
- Immortals with character support
- Ophydian Destroyers
- Skorpekh Destroyers with Skorpekh Lord
- 2x Scarabs
- Lokhust Heavy Destroyers with Lokhust Lord support
- Canoptek Wraiths with Technomancer support
- 2x Canoptek Reanimator
- 2x Triarch Stalker

The matchup problem is not just killing power. The Ork list has to:

- survive the first two Necron shooting activations that matter
- crack either `The Silent King` or the support shell around him
- still have enough OC and bodies left to win primary into reanimation

## Simulation Setup

Both sims use the same context:

- Opponent: the same Necron `Cursed Legion` list
- Mission shell: `Hidden Supplies`
- Deployment: `Search and Destroy`
- Terrain frame: same practical assumptions as the earlier sim
- Orks go first for both test games
- Tactical Missions for both players

These are matchup simulations, not dice-by-dice engine outputs. The useful signal here is comparative performance, not exact card draws.

## Sim 1: List A vs Necrons

### Game Flow

- The Orks spend the first turn staging both transports and the Boyz brick while scoring lightly with utility units.
- The Necrons still blunt one threat with shooting, but `Battlewagon + Ghaz + Meganobz` survives long enough to force a premium mid-board collision.
- On the Waaagh! turn, `Ghaz + Meganobz` and `Warboss + Nobz` hit different parts of the Necron shell instead of dogpiling one target.
- That split pressure is the key:
  - one hammer crushes a support piece or Stalker lane
  - the other clears a midfield holder
  - Boyz keep the mission from slipping away
- The Necrons still trade back well, but they struggle to both preserve `The Silent King` and hold enough objectives.

### Expected Result

- `Orks win 78-70`

### Why

- This list trades more efficiently into elite Necron profiles.
- Ghaz's bodyguard is small enough to move cleanly through terrain and tough enough to matter after one shooting phase.
- The Orks still retain enough OC through Boyz, Beast Snaggas, Grots, and utility units to outscore late.

## Sim 2: List B vs Necrons

### Game Flow

- The `Ghaz + Boyz` unit dominates board space, but it is harder to hide and harder to fully connect through dense central ruins.
- The Necrons use scarabs, Wraiths, and terrain angles to force awkward movement and partial engagements.
- When the Orks commit, the unit still hits hard, but it is easier for the Necrons to peel screens, counter-charge, and isolate secondary threats.
- The Boyz version is noticeably better at primary scoring through rounds 2-3, but worse at actually finishing the Necron anchors.
- By late game, the Orks are still in it, but too much of the killing burden falls onto the smaller Nob and Meganob units.

### Expected Result

- `Necrons win 76-72`

### Why

- Ghaz with Boyz is strong, but into this specific Necron list it is a little too much OC and not quite enough concentrated anti-elite punch.
- The larger footprint makes it easier for the Necron player to control where the real fight happens.
- The Boyz version scores better early, but the Meganobz version converts pressure into kills better.

## Verdict

If the goal is specifically to beat this Necron list, I would choose `List A: Ghaz + Meganobz`.

### Final Ranking

1. `War Horde - Ghaz + Meganobz`
2. `War Horde - Ghaz + Boyz`

### Short Version

- `Ghaz + Boyz` is real and good.
- `Ghaz + Meganobz` is the better answer into this exact Necron army.
- The reason is not raw damage alone; it is compact threat delivery plus better trades into elite Necron profiles.

## Confidence

- Medium.
- I am confident in the relative ranking between the two lists.
- The exact scorelines would move based on tactical draws, terrain micro, and whether the Necron player exposes `The Silent King` too early.
