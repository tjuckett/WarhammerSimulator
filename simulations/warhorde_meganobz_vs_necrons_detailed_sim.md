## Mission Frame

- Primary: `Hidden Supplies`
- Deployment: `Search and Destroy`
- Tactical Missions for both players
- Orks go first
- Terrain assumption: same dense central-tournament style setup used in the earlier reports

## Pre-Game Plan

### Orks

- `Ghaz + 6 Meganobz` start in the `Battlewagon`
- `Warboss + 10 Nobz` start in one `Trukk`
- `5 Nobz` start in the other `Trukk`
- `20 Boyz + Warboss` form the central OC wave
- `Beastboss + 10 Beast Snagga Boyz` take one pressure flank
- second `10 Beast Snagga Boyz` hold for follow-up trading
- `Kommandos` forward deploy onto a safe staging ruin
- `Stormboyz` stay in reserve for late scoring or a pressure drop
- `Gretchin` sit on home and near-home mission jobs

### Necrons

- The Silent King central but screened
- Heavy Destroyers protected with firing angles into the mid-board
- Reanimators and Triarch Stalkers staggered behind the first line
- Wraiths and Skorpekhs poised to counter-charge mid objectives
- Ophydians in reserve

## Win Conditions

### Orks

- Do not trade into scarabs and screens too early
- Force the Necrons to reveal either `The Silent King` or their support shell
- Kill `Reanimators` and at least one `Triarch Stalker` before the mid-game ends
- Keep enough OC bodies alive to hold two-plus objectives into turns 4 and 5

### Necrons

- Strip the transports before Ghaz and Nobz arrive
- Use Wraiths and scarabs to delay the real charges
- Preserve The Silent King until the Ork hammers are fragmented
- Outlast the Waaagh! turn, then win late on reanimation and board control

## Battle Round 1

### Orks Turn 1

- Kommandos take a forward ruin and threaten an early objective swing without overexposing.
- Both Trukks and the Battlewagon advance into layered staging positions behind obscuring terrain.
- The Boyz brick moves up the middle, but deliberately stays behind the transport wall.
- Beast Snaggas pressure the weaker Necron flank and make the Stalker/Reanimator pair there uncomfortable.
- Ork shooting is mostly incidental; the goal is board posture, not damage.
- Tactical play:
  - One utility mission is scored through board presence.
  - The Orks do not spend key resources fishing for low-value early damage.

### Necrons Turn 1

- The Necrons open the shell carefully.
- Heavy Destroyers plus one Stalker focus down a Trukk, exposing the `5 Nobz`.
- The Silent King contributes ranged pressure but stays conservative.
- One Reanimator repairs chip damage from incidental earlier fire.
- Wraiths move into the central lane to threaten a turn-2 body block.
- Scarabs fan out to make landing zones awkward.
- Necrons score lightly and keep the table stable.

### End of Round 1

- Orks are ahead on space.
- Necrons are ahead on attrition efficiency.
- The game state is healthy for the Orks because the `Battlewagon` and one `Nobz Trukk` are still intact.

Score:

- Orks `11`
- Necrons `8`

## Battle Round 2

### Orks Turn 2

- This is the staging turn, not yet the all-in turn.
- The disembarked `5 Nobz` go into a side-trade role instead of trying to frontally crack the castle.
- `Warboss + 10 Nobz` threaten the exposed Wraith lane.
- The Battlewagon moves up to force a response from The Silent King's side.
- Beast Snaggas pressure a `Triarch Stalker` and nearby support.
- Kommandos force the Necrons to commit real resources to a flank objective.
- Ork pressure kills:
  - one `Canoptek Reanimator`
  - one scarab unit
  - significant damage on one `Triarch Stalker`
- The Orks intentionally hold the Waaagh! for round 3.

### Necrons Turn 2

- The Necrons counterpunch before the real Ork hammer lands.
- Wraiths and shooting combine to remove the exposed `5 Nobz`.
- Heavy Destroyers and support crack the second transport lane and chip the Battlewagon hard.
- The Silent King and the remaining support element clean up Kommandos or reduce them to irrelevance.
- Skorpekhs move into a threatening central trade position, daring the Orks to commit.
- The Necrons reclaim some board space, but have now lost one major support piece.

### End of Round 2

- Orks have lost a small trade unit and some mobility.
- Necrons have lost one Reanimator and one of their cheap screens.
- The battlefield is now set for the decisive Waaagh! turn.

Score:

- Orks `24`
- Necrons `22`

## Battle Round 3

### Orks Turn 3: Waaagh!

- The Orks call Waaagh! here, where it matters most.
- `Ghaz + Meganobz` disembark and go for the premium target lane.
- `Warboss + 10 Nobz` slam into the central objective fight.
- `20 Boyz + Warboss` flood onto the mid-board to convert pressure into OC, not just casualties.
- `Stormboyz` arrive to score a side Tactical or threaten the weak Necron back corner.
- Beastboss and Beast Snaggas finish the already-damaged `Triarch Stalker`.

Key combats:

- `Ghaz + Meganobz` maul `The Silent King` package, heavily degrading it and stripping key board control.
- `Warboss + Nobz` delete the Wraith unit or leave it combat-ineffective.
- Boyz pile onto the mid objective and make it difficult for the Necrons to swing primary back immediately.

This is the best turn in the game for the Orks.

### Necrons Turn 3

- The Necrons still hit back hard.
- Skorpekhs and surviving elite pieces pick up the `10 Nobz` or most of them.
- Heavy Destroyers punish whatever remains exposed after the Waaagh! charge.
- Ophydian Destroyers arrive to threaten a home or side objective and force the Grots to play honestly.
- Even with the counterblow, the Necrons are now missing too many support layers for their late game to feel comfortable.

### End of Round 3

- The Orks have taken real losses, but they have broken the Necron shape.
- One Reanimator is dead, one Stalker is dead, Wraiths are gone or functionally gone, and The Silent King is either dead or crippled enough to stop dictating the table.
- This is the swing point where the Orks become favored.

Score:

- Orks `46`
- Necrons `39`

## Battle Round 4

### Orks Turn 4

- The Orks stop trying to table the Necrons and start trying to win.
- Ghaz and surviving Meganobz clean up the most valuable nearby target rather than overchasing.
- Boyz take over the central scoring role.
- Grots and Stormboyz stretch the scoreboard.
- Beast Snaggas trade into whichever support piece is still enabling Necron recovery.
- If the Silent King still lives, he is finished here; if not, this turn is about removing the remaining Heavy Destroyer/Reanimator package.

### Necrons Turn 4

- Necrons still score, but their scoring becomes narrower and more localized.
- Heavy Destroyers and a surviving character package peel away some Ork resources.
- Ophydians or surviving skirmish pieces steal a side objective for one turn.
- The Necron player no longer has enough durable OC to dominate the middle and enough firepower to clear the Ork secondary pieces at the same time.

### End of Round 4

- The game is still live, but the Orks are clearly ahead.
- The Necrons can still punish isolated units, but not enough units remain to recover both board and score.

Score:

- Orks `62`
- Necrons `53`

## Battle Round 5

### Orks Turn 5

- Final turn is about preserving controlled objectives and finishing easy Tactical points.
- Surviving Boyz, Grots, and a battered elite unit hold enough space to keep the lead.
- Any surviving hammer piece picks up one final exposed Necron unit to deny a late swing.

### Necrons Turn 5

- The Necrons post a respectable final turn because their list is still efficient even while damaged.
- They can usually kill one more Ork scoring piece and pick up a secondary.
- What they cannot do anymore is reverse the primary spread created in turns 3 and 4.

## MVPs

### Orks

- `Ghaz + Meganobz`
  - They are the unit that actually converts pressure into elite Necron casualties.
- `20 Boyz`
  - They make the list score after the transports crack.
- `Beastboss + Beast Snaggas`
  - They are excellent at finishing damaged Stalkers/Reanimators and forcing awkward trades.

### Necrons

- `Heavy Destroyers`
  - Best anti-transport and punishment unit in the list.
- `The Silent King`
  - Still warps the game even when he dies.
- `Wraiths`
  - Their real value is slowing the Ork timetable.

## Why This Version Wins

- It does not rely on one giant footprint unit to do everything.
- `Ghaz + Meganobz` are compact enough to deliver through dense terrain and tough enough to survive into the real fight.
- The list still has a proper mission core: `Boyz`, `Grots`, `Kommandos`, `Stormboyz`, and Beast Snaggas.
- Once the Orks remove one Reanimator and one key vehicle/support piece, the Necron castle stops compounding its efficiency.

## Practical Advice If You Play It

- Do not pop the Waaagh! on turn 2 unless the Necron player badly overextends.
- Kill `Reanimators` before chasing pretty targets.
- Use `Boyz` to score, not to be your main killer.
- Do not feed `Ghaz + Meganobz` into scarabs unless it opens a direct follow-up into a premium target.
- Accept that one transport probably dies early; your goal is that not all of them do.
