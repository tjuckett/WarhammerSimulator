# Addendum: Necrons with 3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers

## Important Correction

The Necron list in `lists/necrons/Necrons  - Cursed Legion.json` already appears to include the full `3-model Lokhust Heavy Destroyers` unit.

The strongest evidence in the export is:

- `Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`
- model entry: `Destroyer w/ enmitic exterminator`
- unit cost shown through the export as `165 pts`

That points to the full 3-model unit already being present in the roster, not a smaller version.

## What This Means

The earlier simulations were already close to the correct structural matchup.

However, to be safe, this addendum re-runs the Ork matchup study with the Necron `Lokhust Heavy Destroyers` treated explicitly as:

- a full 3-model unit
- protected by `Lokhust Lord` support
- played as an optimized anti-transport / anti-elite backfield battery

This makes the Necron gunline slightly more punishing in practice, especially into:

- `Trukks`
- `Battlewagon`
- exposed `Meganobz`
- damaged follow-up melee bricks

## Impact On The Matchup

### General Effect

Compared with the earlier study, the Necrons gain:

- more consistent early transport removal
- better punishment after the Waaagh! turn
- stronger late-game cleanup into damaged elite Ork units

The shift is real, but not massive, because the full 3-model Heavy Destroyer package was already implicitly part of the matchup assumptions.

## New Takeaway

With the Necron `3-model Lokhust Heavy Destroyers` played as a fully optimized battery:

- the matchup gets tighter for Orks overall
- `War Horde Meganobz` is still the best all-around Ork answer
- `War Horde Boyz` is still best on wide action-heavy boards
- `Bully Boyz` loses a bit of reliability because the stronger Heavy Destroyer battery punishes compact elite delivery more consistently

## Best Ork Answer Still

If you are building to beat this exact Necron list as now clarified, I would still choose:

- `War Horde`
- `Ghaz + Meganobz`
- real scoring core with `Boyz`, `Gretchin`, `Kommandos`, and `Stormboyz`

## Practical Difference In Play

The biggest gameplay change is not the final list recommendation. It is target priority:

1. The Orks need to pressure or screen the `Lokhust Heavy Destroyers` earlier.
2. Losing both transports too early becomes a much bigger risk.
3. The `Battlewagon` has to be staged more carefully.
4. The Orks should value obscuring and staggered threat timing even more than before.

## Bottom Line

You do not actually need to change the Necron list to get to `3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`; it already looks like it has them.

After re-running the matchup with that assumption made explicit, the Ork side gets slightly worse, but the recommended answer does not change:

- `War Horde Meganobz` remains the best overall counter-build.
