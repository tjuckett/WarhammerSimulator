# All Ork Armies vs Revised Triple-Lokhust Necrons

## Question

Against the revised Necron roster built around:

- `The Silent King`
- `3x3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`
- `Lokhust Lord`
- `Overlord + Plasmancer + Immortals`
- `Technomancer + Wraiths`
- `2x Scarabs`
- `Ophydian Destroyers`
- `2x Canoptek Reanimator`
- `2x Triarch Stalker`

what is the best Ork army build?

## Method

I tested representative `2000-point` Ork builds for each major detachment/archetype family, using the same revised Necron target and the same tournament-style scenario pool.

This is a scenario-study simulation, not a dice-by-dice engine. The point is comparative performance:

- which detachment handles the matchup best
- which ones are traps
- whether any Ork build becomes meaningfully favored

## Ork Builds Tested

### 1. War Horde Balanced Pressure

- `Ghaz + Meganobz`
- `Nobz`
- `20 Boyz`
- `Beast Snaggas`
- `Kommandos`
- `Stormboyz`

### 2. War Horde Wide Pressure

- smaller premium package
- more `Boyz`

Built to survive better into heavy anti-transport fire.

### 3. Green Tide

- multiple `20-Boyz` bricks
- `Painboy`
- `Warboss` support
- very low premium target density

Built to stress mission play and OC.

### 4. Taktikal Brigade

- `Boss Snikrot`
- `3x Kommandos`
- `3x Stormboyz`
- mixed medium threats
- mission and geometry pressure

Built to use the detachment honestly.

### 5. Bully Boyz

- stacked elite melee
- `Nobz/Meganobz`
- compact delivery

Built to maximize elite trade efficiency.

### 6. Dread Mob

- walker and vehicle pressure
- Meks
- ranged/melee hybrid threat package

Built to overload tough targets and create target saturation.

### 7. Kult of Speed

- fast buggies/bikes/transports
- multiple angle pressure

Built to test whether pure speed can out-rotate the Necron gun castle.

### 8. More Dakka!

- shooting-weighted Orks

Built to see whether a volume-fire answer can work.

### 9. Da Big Hunt

## Ranking

### 1. Green Tide

Best overall.

Why it won:

- it minimizes the value of triple `Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`
- it is least dependent on transports surviving
- it forces the Necrons to keep answering OC and side scoring all game
- it survives going second better than the other Ork shells

Where it wins:

- `Terraform`
- `Supply Drop`
- wider layouts
- any game where the Necrons cannot simply hold the center and delete delivery pieces

### 2. War Horde Wide Pressure

Best non-Green Tide option.

Why it did well:

- still keeps one real hammer
- avoids overexposing too many premium targets
- better mission flexibility than the balanced pressure version

Why it lost out:

- not quite enough body flood to fully solve the matchup

### 3. War Horde Balanced Pressure

Still respectable, but no longer the best once the Necron roster is fully optimized around triple Lokhusts.

Why it slipped:

- too much value for the Necron guns to pick up early
- transport disruption matters too much

### 4. Taktikal Brigade

Playable, but not top-tier in this matchup.

Why:

- great on wide, tricky missions
- not enough raw damage conversion when the Necron castle holds together

### 5. Da Big Hunt

Interesting, but not good enough overall.

Why:

- the anti-vehicle plan is attractive on paper
- in practice the Necrons still punish the delivery and the list does not score as well as Green Tide

### 6. Bully Boyz

Good ceiling, bad floor.

Why:

- when it lands, it lands hard
- but triple Lokhusts punish elite delivery too reliably

### 7-9. More Dakka!, Kult of Speed, Dread Mob

These all came out as clear no-go builds into this specific Necron roster.

Why:

- `More Dakka!` does not beat the Necrons at their own gun game
- `Kult of Speed` is too fragile
- `Dread Mob` gives the Necron list exactly the target profile it wants to see

## Mission Family Notes

### Best Ork Missions

- `Supply Drop`
- `Terraform`
- wider `Sweeping Engagement` style games

These reward:

- repeated OC pressure
- action scoring
- lateral board spread

### Worst Ork Missions

- `Scorched Earth`
- dense `Search and Destroy` fights

These reward:

- actually cracking the elite center on schedule
- surviving concentrated firing lanes

## Practical Verdict

### Best Ork Army Into This List

`Green Tide`

This is the cleanest answer if your goal is simply:

- `what gives me the best chance to beat this revised Necron list?`

### Does Any Ork Army Become Comfortably Favored?

`No.`

Even the best-performing build, `Green Tide`, only reached:

- `7-5`
- narrow positive average score

That is enough to call it the best option, but not enough to call it a soft counter.

## Suggested Green Tide Core

The best-performing shape was:

- `Ghazghkull Thraka`
- `3x Warboss`
- `Painboy`
- `4x20 Boyz`
- `1x10 Boyz`
- `1x10 Beast Snagga Boyz`
- `2x10 Kommandos`
- `3x5 Stormboyz`
- `3x11 Gretchin`
- movement/utility enhancements

This shape worked because it:

- gave up fewer premium targets
- kept the mission crowded
- still had one meaningful melee anchor in `Ghaz`

## Bottom Line

After testing the main Ork army styles into the revised triple-Lokhust Necron list:

- `Green Tide` is the best Ork army
- `War Horde Wide Pressure` is second-best
- `War Horde Balanced Pressure` is third
- everything else is worse into this specific matchup

If your goal is to maximize your chance of winning, I would bring `Green Tide`.

If your goal is to keep a more familiar playstyle while still being live, I would bring `War Horde Wide Pressure`.
