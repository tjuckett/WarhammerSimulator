# Taktikal Brigade and Green Tide vs Necrons

## Purpose

This follow-up tests the two most plausible unexplored Ork answers into the Necron matchup:

- `Taktikal Brigade`
- `Green Tide`

These are compared against the earlier best-performing baseline:

- `War Horde Balanced Pressure`

The goal is not just to find a clever alternative, but to see if either detachment is actually better across multiple real mission/layout contexts.

## Target Necron Opponent

This comparison uses the tougher Necron benchmark from the recent follow-up work:

- `The Silent King`
- `Immortals` with support
- `Wraiths`
- `Skorpekhs`
- `Reanimators`
- `Triarch Stalkers`
- heavy backfield shooting emphasis

Where helpful, conclusions are also checked against the more extreme `triple 3-man Lokhust Heavy Destroyer` concept, since that is the hardest realistic version for Orks to solve.

## Ork Candidate 1: Taktikal Brigade

### Why test it

`Taktikal Brigade` is the most interesting unexplored option because it attacks the matchup from the movement, objective, and sequencing side instead of trying to simply brute-force the Necrons off the table.

Things it offers that matter here:

- better utility from `Kommandos` and `Stormboyz`
- real value from `Ded Sneaky`
- strong reposition and follow-through tools from `On To Da Next` and `Taktikal Retreat`
- the ability to make a melee trade and then convert it into mission pressure

`Green Tide` is the other real candidate because it changes the question the Necrons have to answer.

Instead of:

- can the Orks deliver a compact hammer into my elite shell?

## What Won

### Best Overall

`Taktikal Brigade`

This is the first detachment tested that I would now rate above the `War Horde` baseline for this matchup.

Why:

- it keeps enough genuine punch to threaten `The Silent King` and support vehicles
- it is much better at forcing awkward mission decisions on the Necron player
- `Kommandos` and `Stormboyz` do more than just “score a side card”; they actively distort the board state
- the Necron army hates being made to choose between castle integrity and side-objective discipline

### Where It Pulls Ahead

- `Terraform`
- `Supply Drop`
- wider or more rotational missions

It is still good on dense maps, but that is not where it gains the most edge. It pulls ahead when the Necron player cannot just stand still and farm lanes.

## Green Tide Verdict

`Green Tide` is real, but not better overall than `War Horde`.

What it does well:

- overwhelms wide objectives
- performs best on `Terraform` and wide `Supply Drop`
- punishes Necron lists that overinvest in elite gun pieces and underinvest in board spread

Why it is not the top answer:

- it gets worse on dense fight boards
- it is more vulnerable to efficient staged attrition
- it has a harder time converting OC advantage into dead premium Necron units

## Why Taktikal Brigade Edges It

The core reason is that it solves the matchup from a different angle.

`War Horde` asks:

- can I hit harder and more cleanly than the Necrons can absorb?

`Taktikal Brigade` asks:

- can the Necrons both protect their castle and stop me from scoring everywhere I need to score?

That second question is harder for this Necron list to answer.

## Bottom Line

Yes, there is a better Ork army than the first three shells I tried.

The best new answer is:

- `Taktikal Brigade`

It is not dramatically more killy than `War Horde`, but across a wider mission and terrain spread it performs better because it pressures the Necrons where their list is structurally least comfortable: table geometry, side pressure, and action-based scoring.
