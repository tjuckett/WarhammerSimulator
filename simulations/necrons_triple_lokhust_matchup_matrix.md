## What Changes Strategically

Compared with the original list, this version:

- loses cheap screening and side-pressure pieces
- loses some melee support quality
- gains a huge amount of long-range anti-transport and anti-elite firepower

The list becomes much more polarised:

- better at deleting Ork delivery
- worse at wide mission play once the board opens up

## Best Ork Answer Into Triple Lokhust

### Best Overall

`War Horde Balanced Pressure`

It is still the best overall choice, but the margin is slimmer and the matchup is now slightly Necron-favored overall.

Why it stays best:

- it still has the most stable mix of compact pressure and real scoring bodies
- it handles both dense and wide boards better than the alternatives
- it does not rely too heavily on one elite package surviving intact

### Best On Wide Maps

`War Horde Ghaz + Boyz Flood`

This gets relatively better once the mission rewards spread presence over compact delivery.

It does best on:

- `Terraform`
- `Supply Drop` on wider layouts

because the revised Necron list is worse at lateral board control and more committed to a firing castle.

### Biggest Loser

`Bully Boyz`

This version suffers the most from triple Lokhusts because:

- elite delivery is punished more reliably
- losing one transport early is much more damaging
- the revised Necron list has enough backfield firepower to thin the compact kill packages before they fully connect

## How The Matchup Changes

### Before

The earlier Necron version was a more rounded combined-arms elite list.

### After

The triple-Lokhust version becomes:

- more lethal into transports
- more punishing into exposed `Meganobz` and `Nobz`
- weaker at contesting multiple objectives over time

That shifts the optimal Ork plan slightly:

1. You care even more about staging and obscuring.
2. You care slightly less about trying to table the Necrons.
3. You care more about forcing the Necrons to choose between protecting firing lanes and playing the mission.

## Best Practical Ork Plan

If you expect this revised Necron list, I would bring:

- `War Horde`
- `Ghaz + Meganobz`
- `20 Boyz`
- `Kommandos`
- `Stormboyz`
- `2x Gretchin`
- `Battlewagon`
- `2x Trukk`

but I would play it with a stronger mission-first mindset than before.

### Priority Changes

- Do not expose both transports early.
- Pressure `Lokhust Heavy Destroyers` with terrain, threat timing, and angle denial rather than assuming you can just race them.
- Kill `Reanimators` when efficient, but accept that on some boards the Heavy Destroyers are the more important practical problem.
- Wide scoring and late OC matter more now because the revised Necron list gave up cheap board-control pieces to buy the extra guns.

## Bottom Line

Yes, a true `3x3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyer` Necron list is meaningfully stronger into Orks than the original version.

But it does not completely flip the answer.

The best Ork response is still:

- `War Horde`
- compact hammer
- real Boyz/Grot mission core

The difference is that the matchup moves from `slightly Ork-favored with the right build` to `slightly Necron-favored unless the Orks exploit mission spread and terrain well`.
