## Why This Is The Best Version

This list keeps the three things the matchup actually demands:

1. one compact premium hammer
2. one second serious melee lane
3. enough real OC and mission pieces to win the table after the first collision

### Unit Roles

- `Ghaz + 6 Meganobz` in the `Battlewagon`
  - the main premium answer into `The Silent King`, `Triarch Stalkers`, and elite center fights
- `Warboss + 10 Nobz` in one `Trukk`
  - the second hammer that forces the Necrons to split real resources
- `20 Boyz + Warboss`
  - the main OC brick; this unit wins games after the elite pieces trade
- `Beastboss + Beast Snagga Boyz`
  - pressure/trading package into damaged vehicles or exposed support
- second `Beast Snagga Boyz`
  - flank scorer and follow-up trading unit
- `Kommandos`
  - board tax, early objective leverage, and deployment distortion
- `2x Stormboyz`
  - tactical scoring and late objective flips
- `2x Gretchin`
  - home scoring and cheap mission utility

## Why I Did Not Go Harder Into Elite Units

I could make the list more extreme with more `Nobz/Meganobz`, but every time the Ork side gets too elite into this Necron army it starts losing on:

- primary stability
- Tactical flexibility
- recovery after the first Necron shooting swing

This is the best balance point I found.

## Why This Version Wins

### 1. It does not overcommit to one plan

The list can:

- hit hard with `Ghaz + Meganobz`
- hit a second lane with `Nobz`
- still win on OC with `Boyz`, `Grots`, `Stormboyz`, and `Kommandos`

### 2. It does not ask Boyz to do too much

The `20 Boyz` unit scores and stabilizes the mid-board.
It is not your main anti-elite solution.

### 3. It taxes the Necron support shell properly

You do not have to kill everything.
You need to:

- remove at least one `Reanimator`
- remove or cripple one key `Triarch Stalker`
- then trade into whichever premium unit is exposed

### 4. It plays the mission after contact

This is the main thing weaker Ork lists failed to do.
This build still has real endgame units after the first hammer swings.

## How To Pilot It

### Default Embarks

- `Ghaz + 6 Meganobz` in `Battlewagon`
- `Warboss + 10 Nobz` in `Trukk`
- `Beastboss + 10 Beast Snagga Boyz` in `Trukk`
- `20 Boyz + Warboss` on foot

### Primary Target Priority

1. `Reanimators`
2. whichever `Triarch Stalker` is easiest to expose
3. `Wraiths` if they are blocking your timetable
4. `The Silent King` once you can hit him properly

### Biggest Mistakes To Avoid

- calling Waaagh! one turn too early
- feeding `Ghaz + Meganobz` into scarabs or a sacrificial screen
- exposing both transport lanes to the Heavy Destroyers at once
- using `Boyz` as your main damage dealer instead of your scoreboard anchor
