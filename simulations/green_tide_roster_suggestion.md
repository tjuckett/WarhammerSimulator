# Green Tide Roster Suggestion

## Goal

This is the `Green Tide` list I’d suggest specifically into the tougher Necron build with:

- `The Silent King`
- `3x3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`
- `Immortals` with support
- `Wraiths`
- `2x Reanimator`
- `2x Triarch Stalker`

The point is not to out-shoot or out-elite that army.
The point is to:

- reduce premium targets
- flood OC
- make the Necrons answer multiple objective problems at once

## Why This List

### 1. It cuts down on what triple Lokhusts actually want to kill

Triple `Lokhust Heavy Destroyers` love seeing:

- `Battlewagon`
- `Trukks`
- `Meganobz`
- `Nobz`
- elite multi-wound premium units that expose early

This list gives them far fewer of those targets.

### 2. It forces the Necrons to solve bodies instead of just solving delivery

With the earlier `War Horde` versions, a lot of the game hinged on:

- do the transports live
- does the premium hammer arrive

This list asks a different question:

- can the Necrons actually stop four large OC bricks plus side scoring across five turns?

### 3. It leans into the matchups where Orks can keep pace

This list is best on:

- `Supply Drop`
- `Terraform`
- wider objective maps
- games where side pressure matters more than deleting the center immediately

### Utility

- `2x Kommandos` for forward tax and awkward flank pressure
- `3x Stormboyz` for Tactical scoring and late flips
- `3x Gretchin` for cheap OC, home scoring, and mission economy

## What This List Is Trying To Do

It does not want a neat “my hammer into your hammer” game.

It wants:

- one Boyz brick in the middle
- one Boyz brick threatening a second lane
- mission units forcing side commitments
- enough bodies that even if the Necrons delete one unit, the board still feels crowded

## Matchup Plan Into Triple Lokhust Necrons

### Priorities

1. Survive the first two Necron turns without losing control of the mission.
2. Use terrain and staggered pressure so the Heavy Destroyers never get perfect value.
3. Force the Necron player to choose between protecting the castle and answering side objectives.
4. Score through `Stormboyz`, `Kommandos`, and `Grots` while the big bricks pin the table down.

### Do Not

- sprint every Boyz unit into the center at once
- give the Necrons one perfect concentrated shooting turn
- treat Ghaz's unit as your only real threat

### Do

- stage one wave, then a second wave
- use `Kommandos` to pull Necron resources sideways
- keep `Stormboyz` alive for scoring unless a clean late trade is worth it

## Why I Still Keep Ghaz

Even in Green Tide, I still want `Ghaz` because:

- he gives the list one real premium melee threat
- he changes how the Necron player has to screen
- he stops the list from becoming only OC and no punishment

He is not there to solo the whole game.
He is there to make one of the big Boyz bricks genuinely dangerous.

## Final Verdict

If I had to play Orks into the triple-Lokhust Necron list, this is the `Green Tide` army I would start from.

It is not a “hard counter”.
It is the best answer I found because it:

- minimizes the Necron list’s most efficient targets
- plays the mission hardest
- and is the only Ork archetype that got to break-even in the broader simulation work
