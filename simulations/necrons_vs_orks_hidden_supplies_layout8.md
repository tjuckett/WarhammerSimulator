# Necrons vs Orks Sim

## Setup

- Mission: `Hidden Supplies`
- Deployment: `Search and Destroy`
- Terrain: `Layout 8`
- Mission style: `Tactical Missions`
- First turn: `Orks`
- Battlefield size: `Strike Force`

## Source Priority Used

1. `Chapter Approved Tournament Companion` PDF for tournament procedure, terrain-layout guidance, and objective-marker handling.
2. Latest `Chapter Approved 2025-26` mission material on Wahapedia for the mission/deployment references.
3. Wahapedia faction rules and datasheet context for unit roles and likely game flow.

## Important Note

`Hidden Supplies + Search and Destroy` is not one of the Tournament Companion's recommended combinations for `Layout 8`; the packet recommends `Layouts 1, 2, 3, 4, 6` for that mission/deployment pairing. This simulation treats `Layout 8` as a player-agreed house override while keeping the rest of the tournament framework.

## Assumptions

- The roster exports show the units cleanly, but not every pre-game attachment/embark decision in a convenient summary view, so this sim uses reasonable tournament-style assumptions.
- Necron assumptions:
  - Overlord + Plasmancer with Immortals.
  - Skorpekh Lord with Skorpekh Destroyers.
  - Technomancer with Wraiths.
  - Lokhust Lord supporting Lokhust Heavy Destroyers.
  - Ophydian Destroyers start in Reserves.
- Ork assumptions:
  - Beastboss leads Beast Snagga Boyz.
  - One Warboss leads the 10-Nob brick.
  - The Battlewagon carries the main melee punch package.
  - The Trukk carries the smaller Nob package.
  - Stormboyz start in Deep Strike.
- This is a narrative single-game sim, not a strict dice-by-dice engine replay.

## Battlefield Plan

- The Necrons set up conservatively, using the Layout 8 mid-board ruins to screen The Silent King, the Reanimators, and the Heavy Destroyers while threatening two midfield lanes with Wraiths and Skorpekhs.
- The Orks overload one diagonal lane, using the Battlewagon, Trukk, Kommandos, and Ghazghkull pressure to force the issue early and leverage going first.
- Hidden Supplies makes the center less predictable, but the extra objective still favors the Necrons slightly because they can project durable OC and recover attrition better over five rounds.

## Round-by-Round

### Battle Round 1

- The Orks take first turn and push hard into the mid-board without fully exposing Ghazghkull.
- Kommandos stage onto a forward ruin objective and the Trukk/Battlewagon take aggressive positions behind obscuring terrain.
- Ork shooting chips a Reanimator and a Scarab unit, but Layout 8 limits the first-turn spike.
- The Necron reply is efficient:
  - Heavy Destroyers and Triarch Stalker fire strip down the Trukk and expose the smaller Nob unit.
  - The Silent King picks up most of that exposed package.
  - Wraiths and Scarabs take midfield positions while staying layered inside reanimation support.
- End of round state: Necrons already look better on trades because the Ork first turn did not crack a premium target.

Score after BR1:
- Necrons: 12
- Orks: 8

### Battle Round 2

- The Orks call the Waaagh! and commit.
- Ghazghkull's side of the board crashes into the central fight while the remaining Nob brick launches from the Battlewagon.
- The Orks kill a Triarch Stalker, finish one Scarab unit, and badly maul the Wraith brick.
- That swing is real, but it does not fully break the Necron center.
- On the Necron turn:
  - Reanimation blunts the earlier damage.
  - Skorpekhs counter-charge and remove the smaller exposed Ork melee remnants.
  - Heavy Destroyers plus The Silent King cripple the Battlewagon and force Ghaz's unit into the open.
  - Immortals and support fire clear Kommandos off a flank objective.
- End of round state: the Orks hit first, but the Necrons win the counterpunch and stabilize two midfield objectives.

Score after BR2:
- Necrons: 29
- Orks: 23

### Battle Round 3

- This is the key swing turn.
- Stormboyz arrive for the Orks and help pressure a side objective, while Ghazghkull moves in for the main brawl.
- Ghaz and the surviving melee core finally connect hard enough to finish the damaged Wraith unit and threaten the Necron castle.
- The Necrons answer with their biggest activation sequence of the game:
  - Heavy Destroyers and The Silent King bracket Ghazghkull's package.
  - Skorpekhs and a fresh support push finish the exposed Nobz.
  - Ophydian Destroyers arrive from Reserves to flip a weakly held backfield objective and force the Orks to split attention.
- End of round state: the Orks still have pieces, but the Necrons now control tempo and the wider table.

Score after BR3:
- Necrons: 49
- Orks: 38

### Battle Round 4

- The Orks are now playing from behind and leaning on tactical draws plus Ghazghkull's last real push.
- Ghaz kills meaningful material, but the Ork army is too fragmented to retake enough objectives.
- Gretchin hold what they can in the backfield, but they cannot replace the lost midfield pressure.
- The Necrons spend the turn widening the score:
  - Immortals and support units clean up utility infantry.
  - The Silent King survives long enough to dominate a firing lane.
  - Ophydians and remaining utility pieces keep stealing scoring positions.
- End of round state: the Necrons are ahead on primary, ahead on board, and no longer need to overextend.

Score after BR4:
- Necrons: 67
- Orks: 53

### Battle Round 5

- The Orks still generate some late tactical VP and trade off a few last units, but the game is already leaning firmly Necron.
- Necron durable remnants, especially the surviving characters, Immortals, and gun platforms, close the game cleanly.
- The Orks finish with a respectable score because Tactical Missions keep them in it, but they do not reclaim enough primary ground.

## Final Result

- `Necrons win, 82-67`

## Why The Necrons Won

- The Orks went first, but Layout 8 gave the Necrons enough obscuring safety that the opening punch did not land on a premium target.
- The Necron list is better at recovering attrition in a five-round objective game.
- Dual Reanimators, The Silent King's output, and the heavy ranged support punished every Ork unit that had to step into the open.
- Hidden Supplies rewarded sustained midfield presence more than a one-turn alpha melee trade.
- The Orks still posted a playable score, but once their transports and first melee wave were traded down, they ran short on reliable objective control.
