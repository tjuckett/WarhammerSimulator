# Triple Lokhust Necron Roster Suggestion

## Short Answer

No, the earlier `triple Lokhust` version I used for testing was not what I would call the optimal final build.

It was a clean way to force the list to `3x3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers` at `2000`, but it cut too much board texture:

- too few screens
- too little side pressure
- too much reliance on the castle just standing still and deleting things

If I wanted to actually play a `triple Lokhust` Necron list into Orks, I would start from the roster below instead.

## Why I Like This Version More

### 1. It still has the real gunline core

This is the point of the army:

- `3x3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`
- `Lokhust Lord`
- `The Silent King`

That is the main killing package, and it is the part that makes the list scary into Orks.

### 2. It keeps enough board-control tools

The earlier rough version cut too much of the “small game”:

- `Scarabs`
- `Ophydians`
- movement pressure
- cheap screens

This version keeps enough of that that the army is not just a static firing puzzle.

### 3. It keeps the support pieces that actually matter

- `2x Reanimator`
- `2x Triarch Stalker`

Those units are a big part of why Necrons stay annoying even after the first casualties.

### 4. It does not overinvest in the wrong melee package

I like `Wraiths` here more than trying to keep too much of the Skorpekh package.

Why:

- Wraiths are better at movement denial and objective friction
- the list already has enough “kill”
- it needs pieces that buy time for the gunline

## How I’d Organize It

- `Lokhust Lord` sits with one Heavy Destroyer unit as the main anchor battery.
- `The Silent King` plays near the core, not too far forward early.
- `Overlord + Plasmancer + Immortals` hold a protected scoring/fire-support role.
- `Technomancer + Wraiths` take the annoying midfield job.
- `Scarabs` screen and force awkward charges.
- `Ophydians` threaten the side or back objective game.
- `Reanimators` and `Triarch Stalkers` layer support and lane control.

## Is This Optimal?

### Honest answer

I would call this `closer to optimal`, not `proven optimal`.

Why I’m saying that carefully:

- “optimal” depends on what you expect to face
- into Orks specifically, more screens and more board-control are worth a lot
- into some other metas, you might prefer a slightly greedier shooting version

## What I Think Is Actually Optimal About The Archetype

If the goal is to optimize `triple Lokhust` Necrons into Orks, I think the build needs four things:

1. `3x3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`
2. `The Silent King`
3. enough support to keep the castle efficient
4. enough cheap/mobile pieces that the Orks cannot just win on mission spread

That is why I prefer this roster over the earlier stripped-down version.

## What I Would Not Do

I would not make the list even greedier by cutting all the small utility pieces just to jam in more elite damage.

That makes the gunline nastier on paper, but worse at:

- screening
- forcing bad charges
- handling `Stormboyz`, `Kommandos`, or late OC flips
- playing wide missions

## Verdict

So the clean answer is:

- `No, the earlier triple-Lokhust version was not the optimal final makeup.`
- `Yes, this is the version I would suggest instead.`
