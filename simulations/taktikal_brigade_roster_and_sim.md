## Why This Roster Works

This is not the most brutal raw damage list Orks can field, but that is not the point.

It is built to do three things well into Necrons:

1. threaten two real melee lanes
2. overload side-objective discipline with `Kommandos` and `Stormboyz`
3. keep enough OC on the table that the Necrons cannot just win by surviving

The big gain over `War Horde` is not that every unit hits harder. It is that this list turns more of the table into a problem at the same time.

## Pre-Game Plan

### Orks

- One `Kommandos` unit with `Boss Snikrot` stages aggressively but safely.
- The second `Kommandos` unit uses its infiltrate pressure to force the Necrons to respect both flanks.
- `Stormboyz` stay flexible for scoring, counter-pressure, or reserve drops.
- The transport package stages so the Necrons cannot get clean angles on both key delivery pieces at once.
- The `Boyz` brick sits central enough to threaten future primary, but not so far forward that it becomes a free turn-1 target.

### Necrons

- The Silent King anchors the central safe lane.
- Heavy Destroyers and Stalkers set up overlapping anti-transport fire.
- Wraiths and Skorpekhs prepare to screen or counter-charge.
- Reanimators sit where they can keep the castle efficient.

## Battle Round 1

### Orks Turn 1

- The Orks stage first, not commit first.
- `Kommandos` take forward positions that threaten two separate objectives.
- `Battlewagon` and one `Trukk` hide behind the best obscuring lane.
- The second `Trukk` pushes a wider angle to split Necron fire.
- `Boyz` take central real estate without overreaching.
- `Gretchin` and one `Stormboyz` unit start mission scoring early.

### Necrons Turn 1

- Necrons remove one exposed pressure piece, typically a `Trukk` or the more vulnerable `Kommandos` angle.
- Heavy Destroyers strip a transport if they can see it.
- The Silent King stays conservative and avoids becoming a turn-2 trap.
- Wraiths move to block the best Ork route through the middle.

### End of Round 1

- Orks are slightly ahead on space.
- Necrons are slightly ahead on raw efficiency.
- This is acceptable for the Orks because the board is already wider than the Necron player wants it to be.

Score:

- Orks `12`
- Necrons `8`

## Battle Round 2

### Orks Turn 2

- One `Kommandos` unit forces a flank trade and starts to tax Necron objective discipline.
- `Stormboyz` threaten a second angle, making it harder for the Necrons to leave only token defenders on side objectives.
- `Warboss + Nobz` set up for the main punch, but still do not go in unless the target is worth it.
- `Beast Snaggas` pressure a support vehicle or side lane rather than chasing center glory.
- The Orks score efficiently without needing to overcommit their best units.

### Necrons Turn 2

- The Necrons kill the most exposed Ork trading unit and try to peel away side pressure.
- Wraiths or Skorpekhs engage one part of the Ork mid-board plan.
- The castle still hits hard, but each unit used to solve side problems is one less unit reinforcing the center.

### End of Round 2

- The score stays close, but the position begins to favor Orks.
- The Necron player is spending elite output on utility pieces because ignoring them is worse.

Score:

- Orks `27`
- Necrons `23`

## Battle Round 3

### Orks Turn 3: Waaagh!

- This is the swing turn.
- `Ghaz + Meganobz` launch from the `Battlewagon`.
- `Warboss + Nobz` hit the main contested objective.
- `Boyz + Warboss` flood onto the table center and convert the attack into primary pressure.
- One `Stormboyz` unit lands for a scoring or cleanup job.
- `Kommandos` either survive to continue being annoying or have already done enough to distort the deployment geometry.

Key outcomes:

- one `Reanimator` dies
- one `Triarch Stalker` or the `Wraith` screen gets removed
- The Silent King is either heavily bracketed or suddenly exposed to a follow-up

The important part is not necessarily killing everything. It is making the Necrons lose control over where the fighting happens.

### Necrons Turn 3

- The Necrons counter hard.
- Heavy Destroyers and surviving support pieces kill one major Ork hammer.
- Skorpekhs or remaining elites punish an overextended piece.
- But the Necrons are now spread thinner than they want to be, and the Orks have more surviving scoring units than in the `War Horde` version.

### End of Round 3

- The game swings in the Ork direction.
- The Necrons still have teeth, but not enough free resources to both restore order and reclaim every scoring lane.

Score:

- Orks `49`
- Necrons `40`

## Battle Round 4

### Orks Turn 4

- The Orks stop taking ambitious fights and start taking efficient ones.
- `Stormboyz`, `Kommandos`, and `Gretchin` keep the scoreboard turning.
- Surviving `Boyz` anchor primary.
- Any surviving premium melee piece finishes either `The Silent King` or the most important remaining support unit.

### Necrons Turn 4

- Necrons still pick up meaningful kills, but their board shape is compromised.
- If they chase side scoring, they lose center.
- If they protect center, the Orks keep farming the wide table.

### End of Round 4

- The Orks are no longer winning by raw damage.
- They are winning by forcing the Necrons to play too many games at once.

Score:

- Orks `65`
- Necrons `54`

## Battle Round 5

### Orks Turn 5

- Final turn is about preserving objective spread and completing one more tactical score.
- Whatever survives of the elite package only needs to deny a single major Necron recovery lane.

### Necrons Turn 5

- The Necrons score respectably, but the earlier mission bleed is too much to fully recover.

## Why This Won

- `Taktikal Brigade` made the Necron player answer side pressure all game.
- `Ghaz + Meganobz` still provided the compact premium hammer the matchup needs.
- The list did not rely on one giant Boyz brick to do everything.
- Multiple cheap, annoying units created a scoring tax the Necron elite castle struggled to pay.

## Practical Notes

- Do not throw away `Kommandos` for style points; their value is the board problem they create.
- `Stormboyz` are often worth more as tactical scorers than as damage pieces.
- The biggest mistake with this list is playing it like `War Horde`; it wins by pressure geometry, not just by committing first.
