# Can Any Ork Build Beat Triple Lokhust Necrons?

## Question

Can an Ork army be built that is actually favored, or at least above `50%`, into the revised Necron list with:

- `The Silent King`
- `3 units of 3 Lokhust Heavy Destroyers`
- `Immortals` with support
- `Wraiths`
- `2x Reanimator`
- `2x Triarch Stalker`

This is the nastiest realistic version of the matchup we have tested so far.

## Why This Version Is So Hard

The triple-Lokhust version trades away some board texture, but gains a huge amount of:

- anti-transport pressure
- anti-elite punishment
- early activation reliability

For Orks, that means:

- `Battlewagon` and `Trukks` are much less reliable delivery
- compact elite units like `Nobz` and `Meganobz` get punished harder after they commit
- games are much more likely to be decided by whether the Orks can survive turns 1-2 with enough tempo left to matter

## Ork Archetypes Tested

I tested the four most plausible answers:

### 1. War Horde Balanced Pressure

- `Ghaz + Meganobz`
- `Nobz`
- `Boyz`
- `Beast Snaggas`
- `Kommandos`
- `Stormboyz`
- transport shell

Reason to test:

- best all-round answer into the original Necron list

### 2. War Horde Wide Pressure

- less elite concentration
- more `Boyz`, `Stormboyz`, `Kommandos`, `Gretchin`
- only one compact premium hammer

Reason to test:

- if transports die anyway, maybe widen the board and reduce premium target density

### 3. Green Tide

- multiple large `Boyz` bricks
- `Painboy` support
- fewer premium targets

Reason to test:

- tax the Necron castle on OC and mission spread instead of quality trades

### 4. Taktikal Brigade True Mission Play

- `Snikrot`
- `3x Kommandos`
- `3x Stormboyz`
- mixed medium threats
- less dependence on a single frontal hit

Reason to test:

- attack the matchup through geometry and mission flow rather than raw damage

## Results

### War Horde Balanced Pressure

- Record: `4-8`
- Average: `71.8 - 75.6`

Pattern:

- still the best “straight fight” Ork list
- but it loses too much equity when the Necrons pick up transports early

### War Horde Wide Pressure

- Record: `5-7`
- Average: `73.0 - 74.1`

Pattern:

- clearly better than the elite-leaning version on wide maps
- still not over the line overall

### Green Tide

- Record: `6-6`
- Average: `74.3 - 74.2`

Pattern:

- best on `Terraform`, `Supply Drop`, and wider objective games
- worst on `Scorched Earth` and dense center-fight layouts
- much less vulnerable to the “kill the transports and the game is over” problem

### Taktikal Brigade

- Record: `5-7`
- Average: `73.2 - 74.0`

Pattern:

- very playable on mission-heavy boards
- still has trouble cashing in enough hard kills when the Necrons hold their castle together

## Best Candidate

### Winner of the study

`Green Tide`

This was the only Ork archetype that reached break-even across the full spread.

It did not become clearly favored, but it did finally get to `50%`.

## Why Green Tide Did Best

### 1. It reduces premium targets

Triple Lokhusts love:

- `Trukks`
- `Battlewagon`
- `Meganobz`
- `Nobz`

Green Tide gives them fewer high-value shots that radically change the game.

### 2. It changes the scoring problem

The Necrons are very good at deleting one thing.
They are less good at:

- clearing repeated OC floods
- contesting several objectives at once
- stopping action scoring while preserving their castle

### 3. It survives going second better

This was the biggest difference in the study.
Green Tide still dislikes going second, but it loses less hard from it.

## Does Any Ork Build Clear 50%?

### Strict answer

`Yes, barely: Green Tide reached 6-6 in this study.`

### Honest practical answer

`No Ork build looked comfortably favored.`

If your bar is:

- `Can I find an Ork list that is not an underdog?`

then:

- `Yes, Green Tide gets you there.`

If your bar is:

- `Can I find an Ork list that I should expect to beat triple-Lokhust Necrons more often than not with confidence?`

then:

- `No, I did not find that.`

## Best Practical Recommendation

If you must play Orks into triple-Lokhust Necrons, I would now choose:

- `Green Tide`

with priorities:

- multiple large `Boyz` bricks
- `Painboy` support
- `Warboss` support
- `Gretchin` and `Stormboyz` for cheap scoring
- enough secondary threats that the Necrons cannot simply farm the center

## Corrected Matchup Ranking Into Triple Lokhust

1. `Green Tide`
2. `War Horde Wide Pressure`
3. `Taktikal Brigade`
4. `War Horde Balanced Pressure`

## Bottom Line

After a broader final pass, here is the clean answer:

- `Yes, there is an Ork build that can get to 50% into triple-Lokhust Necrons.`
- `That build is Green Tide.`
- `No, I did not find an Ork build that looks clearly favored beyond that.`

So if you want a serious answer rather than a hopeful one:

- `Green Tide is the only version I’d call worth trying if your opponent is on the triple-Lokhust build.`
